Extra questions about the RandomClue.java class:
	The guesser will get the answer correctly within 20 trys, 20/360 of time (5.56% of the time)
	Since 1,1,1 and 6,10,6 are in the domain of all a random answer choice, it does not matter
	which of the options, 1,2, or 3, you choose. 
	
	This algorithm relies entirely on chance. There are 360 different combinations. This 
	strategy does not take advantage of the fact that Assistant Jack tells you which item you
	you guessed incorrectly. The only up side is that you can get the right answer 5 percent of 
	the time. Not a very good strategy.
